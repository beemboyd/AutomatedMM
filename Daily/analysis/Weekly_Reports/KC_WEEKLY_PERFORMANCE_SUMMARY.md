## Conclusion

KC Limit Trending signals showed:

1. **Poor initial adaptation** - Maintained LONG bias for 2 weeks despite bearish market
2. **Eventual alignment** - Week 4 showed correct SHORT bias
3. **Consistent SHORT outperformance** - SHORT signals had 2.2x better success rate
4. **Signal quantity ≠ quality** - More signals didn't predict better performance

**Key Takeaway**: KC signals are trend-following indicators that may lag in rapidly changing markets. The Week 4 shift suggests they eventually adapt but may miss early moves. Use them for:

- Risk management (position sizing)
